Legal Framework

Understand what the law generally focuses on in such cases and why awareness matters.

{% if crime_id == 'tampering-source-documents' %}

Section 65 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Tampering with computer source documents by unauthorized alteration, deletion, concealment, or destruction is a punishable offence. The punishment may extend to 3 years' imprisonment, a fine up to ₹2 lakh, or both.

Section 43 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Unauthorized access, copying, downloading, introducing malware, or altering and damaging data or computer systems is a civil offence and may result in compensation liability.

Section 66 – Information Technology Act, 2000

If an offence under Section 43 is committed dishonestly or fraudulently, it becomes a criminal offence punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years, a fine up to ₹5 lakh, or both.

Tampering with computer source code, modifying system files without authorization, or damaging computer resources is illegal and may result in both civil liability and criminal prosecution under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

{% elif crime_id == 'cyber-terrorism' %}

Section 66F – Information Technology Act, 2000

Defines cyber terrorism, including cyber attacks intended to threaten India’s sovereignty, security, defence, or Critical Information Infrastructure (CII). The offence is punishable with imprisonment for life.

Section 70 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Protects Critical Information Infrastructure (CII). Unauthorized access to or attempts to interfere with protected systems are punishable offences under the Act.

Section 70B – Information Technology Act, 2000

Establishes CERT-In as the national agency for responding to cyber security incidents. Failure to comply with CERT-In’s lawful directions is punishable under the Information Technology Act.

Cyber terrorism, attacks on critical infrastructure, and unauthorized access to protected systems are serious offences under the Information Technology Act, 2000, and may result in severe criminal penalties, including life imprisonment in certain cases.

{% elif crime_id == 'offences-by-intermediaries' %}

Section 69A(3) – Information Technology Act, 2000

Failure to comply with a lawful Government order to block public access to online information is a punishable offence. The punishment may extend to 7 years' imprisonment and a fine.

Section 69 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Failure to assist lawful interception, monitoring, or decryption of information when legally directed by the Government is punishable with imprisonment up to 7 years and a fine.

Section 70B – Information Technology Act, 2000

Failure to comply with directions issued by CERT-In, including mandatory cyber incident reporting, is punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year, a fine up to ₹1 lakh, or both.

Section 67C – Information Technology Act, 2000

Intermediaries that fail to preserve and retain specified information or logs as required by law are punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years, a fine, or both.

Intermediaries must comply with lawful government directions, report cyber security incidents to CERT-In, and preserve required digital records. Non-compliance may result in criminal penalties under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

{% elif crime_id == 'dishonest-receiving-computer-resource' %}

Section 66B – Information Technology Act, 2000

Punishes anyone who dishonestly receives or retains a stolen computer resource or communication device, knowing or having reason to believe it is stolen. The punishment may extend to 3 years' imprisonment, a fine up to ₹1 lakh, or both.

Section 411 – Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023

Punishes any person who dishonestly receives or retains stolen property while knowing or having reason to believe that it is stolen.

Section 317 – Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023

Covers the offence of theft, including the unlawful taking of a computer resource, communication device, or other movable property without the owner’s consent.

Knowingly receiving, possessing, or using a stolen computer resource or communication device is a criminal offence under Indian law and may result in imprisonment, fines, or both. Always verify the lawful ownership of electronic devices before purchasing or using them.
