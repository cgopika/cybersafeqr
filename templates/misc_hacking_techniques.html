Legal Framework

Indian law applies to unauthorized use and misuse of computer systems.

Section 66C – Information Technology Act, 2000

Punishes identity theft, including the illegal use of another person's password, digital signature, or unique electronic identity. The punishment may extend to 3 years' imprisonment, a fine up to ₹1 lakh, or both.

Section 66D – Information Technology Act, 2000

Punishes cheating by personation using computer resources, such as online scams, fake identities, or fraudulent digital impersonation. The punishment may extend to 3 years' imprisonment, a fine up to ₹1 lakh, or both.

Section 43 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Unauthorized access, downloading, copying, introducing malware, or causing damage to computer systems is a civil offence and may result in compensation liability.

Section 66 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Hacking or unauthorized access carried out dishonestly or fraudulently is a criminal offence punishable with imprisonment of up to 3 years, a fine, or both.

Unauthorized hacking, identity theft, digital impersonation, and other computer-related offences are punishable under the Information Technology Act, 2000. Always access computer systems and digital accounts only with proper authorization.
