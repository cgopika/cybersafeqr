How Data Theft Happens

Malware Installed -> Device Scanned -> Sensitive Data Collected -> Data Sent to Attacker -> Victim Suffers Loss

What Can Be Stolen

Passwords

Login details that unlock accounts.

Banking Details

Card numbers and account information.

Aadhaar/PAN

Official identity information used for fraud.

Email Accounts

Access to email can lead to more account takeovers.

Photos and Documents

Private files can be copied or misused.

Saved Browser Data

Autofill and cookies can reveal account sessions.

Warning Signs

  • Unknown login alerts
  • Unexpected password reset emails
  • Strange bank transactions
  • Missing or modified files
  • Slow device performance
  • Unknown apps installed

Consequences

Identity Theft

Attackers can use your identity without permission.

Financial Loss

Accounts and cards may be drained or charged.

Account Takeover

Your online accounts can be hijacked.

Privacy Breach

Private messages and files can be exposed.

Reputation Damage

Stolen data can be used against you.

Blackmail

Attackers may demand money to keep stolen data private.

Protection Tips

  • Use strong passwords
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Download only from trusted websites
  • Keep software updated
  • Use antivirus
  • Backup important files

Legal Framework

Section 43 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Unauthorized access, copying, downloading, or extracting data from a computer system without permission is a civil offence and may result in compensation liability.

Section 66 – Information Technology Act, 2000

If data theft is committed dishonestly or fraudulently, it becomes a criminal offence punishable with imprisonment of up to 3 years, a fine, or both.

Section 72 – Information Technology Act, 2000

Punishes any person who, without consent, accesses or discloses electronic information in breach of confidentiality or privacy. Punishment may extend to 2 years' imprisonment, a fine up to ₹1 lakh, or both.

Accessing, copying, or disclosing another person's confidential electronic data without authorization is illegal and may lead to criminal penalties under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

CyberBot Tip

Malware can steal data silently. If your device behaves unusually, disconnect it from the internet and run a security scan.
